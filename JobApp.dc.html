PRODUCT DESIGNER CHALLENGE
The Job page,
rethought.

By: Leonardo Gamba.

Try the flow
On the Jobs screen, jobs are organized by status so each card reflects its stage in the commercial cycle. Dropdowns keep this simple to navigate, but there's clear room to group and visualize this data more powerfully, giving a meaningful view of the entire company sales cycle.

Note: Only the first card in Leads and the first card in Estimating are operational — the rest are illustrative sample data.
My process
1
Analyze the UI. Identified usability issues: missing breadcrumbs/wayfinding, weak visual hierarchy, lack of clear design patterns.
2
Create the prompt. Based on requirements and findings, emphasizing design systems, usability, and intuitive UI patterns.
3
Clarify the selling process. Displayed each stage clearly, evolving with information until the sale is closed.
Key decisions
→ Category‑based filtering. Products are filtered by category so the company cycle is instantly readable; the Jobs screen evolves into a potential dashboard.
→ Two‑tab Jobs/Estimating screen. One tab for actions and tasks, another for project overview. Users can update and add information until ready to move to the next stage.
→ Gamified lifecycle completion. A standardized flow with light gamification encourages users to complete the product lifecycle.
